## About Me

I'm a sociologist exploring how digital technologies reshape social organization and relationships. Currently pursuing my Master's degree, I conduct fieldwork examining emerging organizational forms—particularly how decentralized systems transform trust-building, skill-sharing, and community formation in rural contexts.

My research interests lie at the intersection of **organizational sociology** and **technology sociology**, with a specific focus on understanding how technologies, organizations, and social relationships co-evolve and mutually shape each other.

---

## Research Interests

- 🏛️ **Organizational Forms & Governance** — How decentralized autonomous organizations (DAOs) operate in real-world settings; bridging between digital governance ideals and lived social practices
    
- 🔗 **Technology-Mediated Social Relations** — How digital technologies act as intermediaries in trust-building, skill-sharing, and community maintenance
    
- 👥 **Small Communities & Identity** — Boundary formation, reputation mechanisms, and social differentiation within emerging grassroots communities
    
- 💼 **Skills, Value & Exchange** — Skill-sharing as both economic practice and relational infrastructure; how competencies become signals of group belonging
    
- 🏘️ **Rural Digital Transitions** — How rural communities adapt and reimagine new organizational models; tensions between centralized/decentralized logics in local contexts
